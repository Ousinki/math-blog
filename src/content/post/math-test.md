# Math Formula Test

This post demonstrates how to use LaTeX math formulas in Astro Cactus blog.

## Inline Math

Here is an inline formula: $E = mc^2$, also known as Einstein's mass-energy equivalence.

The quadratic formula is $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$.

## Block Math

Here is a display formula:

$$
\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2} dx = \sqrt{\pi}
$$

## Maxwell's Equations

The four fundamental equations of electromagnetism:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} &= \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0} \\
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{B} &= 0 \\
\nabla \times \mathbf{E} &= -\frac{\partial \mathbf{B}}{\partial t} \\
\nabla \times \mathbf{B} &= \mu_0\mathbf{J} + \mu_0\varepsilon_0\frac{\partial \mathbf{E}}{\partial t}
\end{aligned}
$$

## More Examples

The Pythagorean theorem: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$

The sum of a geometric series:

$$
S_n = a\frac{1-r^n}{1-r}, \quad r \neq 1
$$

Euler's identity (the most beautiful equation in mathematics):

$$
e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0
$$
